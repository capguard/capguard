By Sarah Jenkins | January 4, 2026

The tomato is the edible berry of the plant Solanum lycopersicum, commonly known as the tomato plant. It originated in western South America and Central America.

Origins and Discovery üåç

Although widely associated with Italian cuisine today, the tomato was actually cultivated by the Aztecs and Incas as early as 700 AD. When Spanish conquistadors brought the fruit back to Europe, it was initially met with suspicion.

"For nearly 200 years, many Europeans believed the tomato was poisonous, referring to it as the 'Poison Apple' because aristocrats often got sick after eating them."

Tomatoes are rich in Vitamin C and Lycopene, making them a staple in healthy diets worldwide.
